A diffusion model paints an image by starting from pure noise and denoising it step by
step toward something that matches a text prompt. Turbo variants distill this down to a
single step, trading a little fidelity for a large speed gain — ideal when you want many
small images fast rather than one slow masterpiece.

Splitting a large canvas into a grid of tiles and rendering each tile on its own worker
is how you turn one big slow job into many small fast ones running in parallel. The
canvas fills in tile by tile as each worker reports back, a live picture of the fan-out.
