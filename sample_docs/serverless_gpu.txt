Serverless GPU compute means you do not keep a machine running. Workers spin up when a
job arrives and scale back to zero the moment the queue is empty. You pay only for the
seconds a worker is actually computing. This is the opposite of renting a dedicated GPU
box by the hour, where you pay even while it sits idle overnight.

The hard part serverless solves is bursty, parallel work: rendering a thousand image
tiles, embedding a corpus of documents, transcoding a video. Each unit of work goes to
its own worker, all of them run at once, and then the whole pool evaporates.
